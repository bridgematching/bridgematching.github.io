In the past three years, diffusion models have emerged as the state-of-the-art method in generative modeling with applications ranging from text-to-image generation to 3D modeling. However, while this framework has seen huge success in image, video, 3D and audio applications, extending diffusion models outside of their original Euclidean generative modeling setting remains challenging. To circumvent some of these issues, several new methods have recently and independently been proposed. As these alternatives now flourish in the literature with great experimental success, this workshop aims at bringing together the different communities working on these topics. Strengthening the connections between the different actors in this field will open new possibilities for interdisciplinary research areas and provide the ground for a unification of the existing frameworks.

The workshop will be held on July 26/27th at ICML's conference center in Vienna - we look forward to seeing you in Vienna!

We invite researchers in diffusion models, flow matching, stochastic interpolants to submit their latest works to our workshop. Topics include (but not limited to) the following topics:
  • Schroedinger Bridges
  • Flow Matching
  • Stochastic Interpolants
